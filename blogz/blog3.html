Blog post week 3

Alignment

User Alignment - designing and structuring my website to meet the needs, expectations, and behaviors of the target users.

My current main goal is to create a website as a new platform for my art as a portfolio and product. Additionally, I aim to achieve a good mark for my interactive media class, planning to have a responsive, accessible, and concise artistic website.

Ultimately, I want my users to see my art and convey it in a creative way that highlights the art of web design.

My Users

What I want my users to do:

  • To view my portfolio.
  • To engage with my art, understanding its significance and meaning.
  • To contact me for commissions and collaborations.
  • To connect with my social media.
  • To purchase artwork.
  • To be aware of any potential events or projects I am involved in or working on.

Who is my target audience?

As an artist, my target audience for my website could include art enthusiasts, collectors, potential clients, galleries, and fans of my work. They are likely on my website to explore and appreciate my artwork, learn more about me as an artist, and potentially purchase or commission artwork.

What do they want to see?

My audience wants to see a visually appealing and well-organized portfolio showcasing my artwork. They may also be interested in learning about my artistic process, inspirations, and upcoming events or exhibitions. Additionally, they may want to easily contact me for inquiries or collaborations.

Reflection

This week, I found myself grappling with a significant challenge: the lack of visual elements in the material we were studying. As someone who thrives on visual creativity and design, I struggled to find enjoyment in the process of building this part of my website. The theoretical and textual focus of the readings didn't provide the inspiration I needed to visualize my end goal. It felt as though I was trying to build something without a clear picture of what it should look like, making the task feel daunting and unfulfilling.

My primary passion lies in creating visually engaging content that speaks to my artistic vision. Without any visual references or elements to guide my design process, I found it difficult to stay motivated and see the project through to completion. This experience has taught me that while understanding the underlying concepts and structures of web design is important, I need to find ways to incorporate visual creativity into every step of my work. Itâ€™s the visual aspects that truly ignite my passion and drive me to create compelling and interactive media.

In the future, I hope to strike a better balance between the technical and the visual components of web design. I plan to integrate more visual elements and inspiration into my workflow, even when the primary focus of the task is more technical or textual. This will help me maintain my enthusiasm and ensure that my projects reflect both my artistic talents and my understanding of web design principles.
