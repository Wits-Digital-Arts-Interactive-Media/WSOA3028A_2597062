Style Guide

Color Palette

I want the website to feel warm and relaxing, so I will use pastel colours and try to have warm earth tones and some green. I want to have an artwork on each page and use maybe 1 or two colours from that to create an even blend.

Imagery

The artwork will be the main focus of my website. I will ensure that the images of the artwork are high-quality and are displayed prominently. The artwork images will be complemented by my chosen color palette to create a cohesive look and feel.

Layout

The website layout will be designed in a way that each section is distinct yet part of a cohesive whole. This will be achieved by using different colors for each section as per my color palette. The layout will be clean and uncluttered, ensuring the artwork remains the focal point.

Fonts

The Amasis MT Pro Black font is like a friendly face in the crowd. It’s a slab serif font, which means it has these little feet at the end of the strokes. But unlike other slab serifs that are more rigid and geometric, Amasis MT Pro Black is more laid-back and organic. It’s bold and stands out, so it’s great for headlines or anything you want to pop. Even though it’s a modern font, it has a timeless feel to it.

Readability and Legibility

One of the best things about this font is that it’s super easy to read, whether it’s on a printed page or a digital screen. Plus, it has a bunch of glyphs, so it can handle a wide range of characters.

Colours

My website will feature a warm, earthy, and natural colour palette. These colours, inspired by nature, will include shades of brown, green, red, and beige.

Colour Theory and Relationships

In terms of colour theory, my chosen palette is quite harmonious. The warm earth tones are analogous, meaning they are next to each other on the colour wheel. This creates a pleasing harmony and cohesiveness. The green, depending on its hue, can act as a complementary colour to the reds, providing a nice contrast and making my website visually interesting.

Composition

The layout of my website will be clean and uncluttered. Each section will have its own distinct colour, creating a visually interesting composition that guides the user’s eye through the site.

Hierarchy, Balance, Harmony

The design of my website will maintain a balance between different elements, creating a sense of harmony. The hierarchy of information will be clearly defined, guiding users intuitively through the content.

Interface Elements - The Visual Representations

Interface elements such as buttons, icons, and menus will be designed to be intuitive and user-friendly. They will be visually consistent with the overall design aesthetic of the website.

Shape, Colour, Position

The shape, colour, and position of each element on my website will be carefully considered to create a cohesive and engaging user experience. The artwork will act decorative but not take away from the UI. I want to have an artwork on each page and have my website feel like a gallery.
