Ethics, UI, UX, and Interaction

Interaction on the web is structured through the UI and UX. Select a South African website or a website offering services in South Africa that interests you. Using the readings and lectures as a starting point, present a detailed analysis of the UI and UX and deliberate on the ethics of the interaction design. You are expected to research independently and draw from more than one resource in your discussion. All academic writing standards will apply. Make sure that your work is correctly referenced in either Harvard or IEEE. Max 2000 words

Essay 2 Brief:

Resources:

  • Google lays off hundreds of ‘Core’ employees, moves some positions to India and Mexico
  • Amrute, S. (2019) ‘Tech Colonialism Today’. RHODE ISLAND, 10 November.

In “Tech Colonialism Today” (2019), Sareeta Amrute outlines the (re)production of colonial relationships as they play out in digital technologies. They discuss digital technologies as hierarchical, extractive, exploitative, resulting in uneven consequences, and displaying malevolent paternalism, giving clear examples of each instance. Using Google’s decision as a starting point discuss the ways in which digital colonialism impacts us as developers in South Africa today.

Suggested points to cover:

  1. Discuss digital (or tech) colonialism using Amrute’s article as a framework.
  2. Relate it to the labour situation in global software development (the Google article is an example, but you need to do more independent research into this and similar instances)
  3. Relate that to the South African software development industry (you will need to do your own research here).
  4. Close with your understanding of your place in the sector and your role in decoloniality.

You are expected to research independently and draw from more than one resource in your discussion. All academic writing standards will apply. Make sure that your work is correctly referenced in either Harvard or IEEE. Max 2000 words
