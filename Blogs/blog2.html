Week 2 Reading

For week 2, I did a reading called ‘You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media (1991)’ by Moulthrop,S (2003). You can find the reading here.

What is Hypertext?

The reading is about Hypertext, which is a kind of specially-formatted text that provides a link to other content. Unlike linear text (such as a book), hypertext allows you to immediately access text in various electronic sources by clicking on hyperlinks. You can learn more about hypertext here.

Relevance to Today's Internet

The reading talks about how technology has changed our habits, particularly in how we interact with hyperlinks. Hypertext was once thought to replace books, providing a source of information where one can move to different sources of information. This ability to learn in a non-linear way is reflected in our modern use of technology.

When we look at our interests, we can expand our knowledge based on patterns, such as hashtags or search engine results. These are prevalent on social media sites. However, while instant access to knowledge is convenient, it also contributes to a shorter attention span. The ability to consume multiple streams of information at once makes us less immersed in the technology we use. The nonlinear nature of hyperlinks can cause people to go down rabbit holes, sometimes to expand knowledge, but other times as a distraction.

Additionally, apps that act as browsers, like Instagram, tend to centralize information around specific themes. For example, Instagram is often used for entertainment, so it may shadow ban certain agendas or concepts.
