Week 2 Blog Post

Week 2 Reading

For week 2, I did a reading called ‘You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media (1991)’ by Moulthrop,S (2003). You can find the reading here.

What is Hypertext?

The reading is about Hypertext, which is a kind of specially-formatted text that provides a link to other content. Unlike linear text (such as a book), hypertext allows you to immediately access text in various electronic sources by clicking on hyperlinks. You can learn more about hypertext here.

Relevance to Today's Internet

The reading talks about how technology has changed our habits, particularly in how we interact with hyperlinks. Hypertext was once thought to replace books, providing a source of information where one can move to different sources of information. This ability to learn in a non-linear way is reflected in our modern use of technology.

When we look at our interests, we can expand our knowledge based on patterns, such as hashtags or search engine results. These are prevalent on social media sites. However, while instant access to knowledge is convenient, it also contributes to a shorter attention span. The ability to consume multiple streams of information at once makes us less immersed in the technology we use. The nonlinear nature of hyperlinks can cause people to go down rabbit holes, sometimes to expand knowledge, but other times as a distraction.

Additionally, apps that act as browsers, like Instagram, tend to centralize information around specific themes. For example, Instagram is often used for entertainment, so it may shadow ban certain agendas or concepts.

Week 3 Blog Post

Alignment

User Alignment- is designing and structuring my website in a way that meets the needs, expectations, and behaviors of the target users.

My current main goal is to create a website as a new platform for my art as a portfolio and as a product. Besides that, I also want to get a good mark for my interactive media class. So, I plan to have a responsive, accessible, concise artistic website.

What I want my users to see ultimately is my art, and I want to convey my art in a creative way that highlights the art of web design.

My Users

What I want my users to do:

  • To view my portfolio.
  • To engage with my art, like understanding its significance and meaning.
  • To be able to contact me for commissions and or collaborations.
  • To connect with my social media.
  • To be able to purchase artwork.
  • To be aware of any potential events or projects I am in or working on.

Who is my target audience?

As an artist, my target audience for your website could include art enthusiasts, collectors, potential clients, galleries, and fans of my work. They are likely on my website to explore and appreciate your artwork, learn more about you as an artist, and potentially purchase or commission artwork.

What do they want to see?

My audience wants to see a visually appealing and well-organized portfolio showcasing my artwork. They may also be interested in learning about my artistic process, inspirations, and upcoming events or exhibitions. Additionally, they may want to easily contact me for inquiries or collaborations.
